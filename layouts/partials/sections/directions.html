Directions

Embodied Perception

Perception is crucial for embodied agents. We aim not only for agents to "see" their surroundings but to develop a deeper "understanding" of the environment across spatial, temporal, and semantic dimensions. A key task is open-vocabulary object detection and segmentation, which focuses on aligning language and vision modalities to enable models to recognize and understand objects they have never encountered before.

Decision Making and Planning

Perception capabilities enable intelligent agents to observe the world, while decision-making and planning abilities allow agents to connect their observations with concepts and knowledge in their minds, enabling appropriate behaviors for task completion, such as goal-seeking and following human language instructions. Additionally, localization, mapping, and obstacle avoidance are essential tasks in robot navigation, allowing robots to determine their position, build global maps, avoid environmental obstacles, and achieve efficient path planning during exploration in unknown environments.

Embodied AI x UAVs

Visit Project Website →

Traditional UAV systems often rely on static planning and manual remote control, which limits robust perception and decision-making in complex environments. In contrast, aerial embodied intelligence integrates foundation model vision language understanding with autonomous policy planning, enabling UAVs to perceive tasks, plan, and act in dynamic, unstructured settings. We build aerial embodied agents from simulation to real-world deployment, advancing aerial robots that can see, think, fly, and collaborate. One example is vision language navigation, where UAVs use visual understanding to perform text guided target search.

Embodied AI x Satellites

Imaging satellite constellations are significant for Earth surface observation, transcending traditional observational limitations and offering unprecedented flexibility. However, scheduling and planning under large-scale scenarios, dynamic environments, and stringent constraints is fundamentally an NP-hard problem. Existing solutions often oversimplify complex real-world conditions, limiting practical performance. We are committed to building a unified framework that integrates benchmark test suites and scheduling models to drive innovation in imaging satellite constellation scheduling.
